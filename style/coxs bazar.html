Cox's Bazar


Cox's Bazar, located in southeastern Bangladesh, is a place of breathtaking natural beauty and wonder. It's best known for having the longest natural sea beach in the world, stretching over 120 kilometers along the Bay of Bengal. Here are some highlights of its

🌊 Endless Blue Horizon The vast stretch of golden sand meeting the endless blue waves creates a magical view, especially during sunrise and sunset, when the sky turns into shades of orange, pink, and red.


🏝️ Inani and Himchari Beaches Inani Beach is famous for its coral stones and crystal-clear water, while Himchari is known for its green hills and waterfalls near the beach—rare and beautiful combinations of mountain and sea.


🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Local Culture The friendly local people, including the traditional lifestyle of the Rakhine community, add cultural beauty to the region. Their handmade crafts and foods give tourists a warm welcome


🌅 Peaceful and Calm If you walk along the quieter parts of the beach, you’ll hear nothing but the waves, wind, and seagulls—a perfect place to relax, think, or just enjoy nature.


🐢 Marine Life and Nature Cox’s Bazar is also close to places like Saint Martin’s Island, which has beautiful coral reefs and crystal-clear waters, and Teknaf, home to part of the Sundarbans mangrove forest and wildlife.
